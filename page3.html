Chapter 2

January, 1906
Village of Morbach, Rhine Province, Kingdom of Prussia

"So this is all that's left, huh?"

A young, slender woman stood in the doorway, holding a notebook. She wore a double-breasted black military jacket, gold buttons catching what little light the room offered, light green epaulettes at her shoulders. A Mauser at her hip. Two short daggers at her back.

She stepped inside slowly. The room was cold and dusty, a thin layer of grey settled over everything. The desk, the bed frame, the windowsill. Untouched, as though no one had set foot here in a month. Maybe longer.

"Come on, Alistair. Surely you left something I can use."

The man who followed her in had to duck under the door frame. Dark skinned, broad shouldered, blue eyed. He wore the same black uniform, a spear across his back alongside his Mauser.

"It's no use," he said. "The woman who runs this place won't speak. Nobody will. I asked three people on the street and got nothing. They looked straight through me."

She didn't respond. She was dragging a finger along the desk, cutting a clean line through the dust.

"Sometime in the last three months his trail went cold," she said quietly. "He was here. This was his room. And then nothing."

The man glanced around. The untouched blanket. The unburned candle.

"I've booked us a room for the night. We should set up and start properly in the morning."

She closed the notebook. She was turning for the door when something stopped her.

In the corner of the room, half tucked beneath the leg of the bed, lay a journal. Or what remained of one. The cover was torn, the pages crumpled and splayed open like a broken wing. She crouched down and reached for it.

A scream split the night.

She was on her feet before the sound had finished. The man was already moving, spear in hand, and she fell in a step behind him, one hand on her Mauser as they clattered down the narrow staircase and out through the tavern door into the cold dark air.
